# Output Visualizations - Atlantic Canada Housing Analysis

This folder contains all the graphs generated by the project. Below is an explanation of each visualization and what it reveals about the data.

---## 📈 **MAIN DASHBOARD**

### `real_housing_visualizations.png`
**A 4-panel dashboard showing different views of the data**

| Panel | What It Shows | Key Insight |
|-------|---------------|-------------|
| **Top Left** | Housing starts over time (2015-2022) | Nova Scotia and New Brunswick are climbing steeply while Newfoundland stays flat |
| **Top Right** | Vacancy rates by city | Halifax has the lowest vacancy rate, indicating a tight rental market |
| **Bottom Left** | Average rent by city | Halifax is the most expensive rental market, explaining its 95% feature importance |
| **Bottom Right** | Price distribution for selected cities | Shows the range of housing prices in major markets |

**Why this matters:** This dashboard provides a comprehensive overview of Atlantic Canada's housing market at aance.

## 📉 **TREND ANALYSIS**

### `real_housing_trends.png`
**4-quarter moving averages of housing starts**

| Province | Trend | Interpretation |
|----------|-------|----------------|
| Nova Scotia | Strong upward slope | Consistent growth driven by Halifax migration |
| New Brunswick | Steep upward slope | Fastest growing market in Atlantic Canada |
| PEI | Moderate upward slope | Steady growth from remote workers and tourism |
| Newfoundland | Flat/declining | Population loss and economic challenges |

**Why this matters:** Moving averages smooth out seasonal fluctuations to revearue long-term trends.

## 🎯 **MODEL PERFORMANCE**

**Predictions vs Actual plot (R² = 0.990)**

| Element | What It Shows |

| **Red dashed line** | Perfect prediction line (where prediction = actual) |
| **Green dots** | Each dot represents one prediction |

## 🔍 **FEATURE IMPORTANCE**

### `feature_importance.png`
**Top factors driving housing starts**

| Rank | Factor | Importance | Meaning |
|:----:|--------|------------|---------|
| **1** | `city_Halifax` | 95% | Being in Halifax is the SINGLE most important factor |
| **2** | `vacancy_rate` | 2% | Market tightness has small but measurable impact |
| **3** | `year` | 1% | Time trend matters slightly |
| **4** | Other cities | ~1% each | Location dominates all other factors |

**Key Insight:** Halifax's housing market is completely different from the rest of Atlic Canada - it accounts for 95% of predictive power!
